Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made of layers of wide pasta sheets, rich meat or vegetable sauce (often with béchamel or ricotta), and melted cheese, typically mozzarella and Parmesan. It’s baked until bubbly and golden. This hearty, comforting dish is perfect for feeding a crowd or for leftovers.

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef or pork (or a mix)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 22 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28oz/800g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6oz/170g) tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce (optional)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 15 oz (425g) ricotta cheese
  • 1.5 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 9-12 lasagna noodles (cooked or no-boil variety)
  1. Make bechamel:

    Melt butter, add flour, and cook 2-3 min. Gradually whisk in warm milk until smooth. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Cook until thick

  2. Add filling:

    Mix in ham, chicken, or fish. Cook 2-3 min. Let the mixture cool, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours.

  3. Shape croquetas:

    Form the chilled mixture into small cylinders or balls

  4. Coat:

    Dip each in beaten egg, then coat with breadcrumbs.

  5. Fry:

    Heat oil in a pan. fry croquetas until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.
