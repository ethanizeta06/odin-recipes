Tortilla

A Spanish tortilla (or tortilla de patatas) is a traditional Spanish dish made of eggs, potatoes, and sometimes onions. The potatoes are thinly sliced, fried, and then mixed with beaten eggs, and the mixture is cooked into a thick, round omelet. Itâ€™s typically served at room temperature, as a tapa or main dish, and can be enjoyed warm or cold. Simple yet delicious, it's a staple in Spanish cuisine.

  • 4-6 medium potatoes
  • 1 medium onion (optional)
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  1. Prepare potatoes and onions:

    Thinly slice the potatoes (and onions, if using).

  2. Fry potatoes and onions:

    Heat oil in a pan and ry potatoes (and onions) until tender and golden, about 15-20 minutes. Drain excess oil and season with salt.

  3. Whisk eggs:

    Beat the eggs with salt and pepper

  4. Combine eggs and potatoes

    Mix the fried potatoes and onions with the beaten eggs. Let is sit for 5-10 minutes

  5. Serve:

    Let it cool slightly before slicing and serving. Enjoy warm or at room temperature!
