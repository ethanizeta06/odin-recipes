Croqueta

Description

Spanish Croquetas are creamy, deep-fried tapas made from a thick béchamel sauce mixed with ingredients like ham (croquetas de jamón), chicken, or fish. The mixture is chilled, shaped into small cylinders or balls, coated in breadcrumbs, and then fried until golden brown. They have a crispy exterior and a soft, rich interior, making them a popular appetizer or snack in Spanish cuisine.

  • 1/4 cup (60g) unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup (60g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups (500ml) whole milk, warmed
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped Serrano or Iberian ham (jamón)
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 to 1.5 cups breadcrumbs (panko or regular)
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  1. Make bechamel:

    Melt butter, add flour, and cook 2-3 min. Gradually whisk in warm milk until smooth. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Cook until thick

  2. Add filling:

    Mix in ham, chicken, or fish. Cook 2-3 min. Let the mixture cool, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours.

  3. Shape croquetas:

    Form the chilled mixture into small cylinders or balls

  4. Coat:

    Dip each in beaten egg, then coat with breadcrumbs.

  5. Fry:

    Heat oil in a pan. fry croquetas until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.
